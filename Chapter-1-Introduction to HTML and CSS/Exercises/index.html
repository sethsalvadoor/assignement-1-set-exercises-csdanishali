Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a beloved Filipino stew known for its rich and creamy peanut sauce. It’s traditionally made with oxtail, vegetables like eggplant and string beans, and sometimes tripe or pork. Served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) on the side, it’s a hearty and flavorful dish that’s perfect for special occasions or Sunday family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs oxtail (or beef shank, tripes, or pork hock)
  • 1 banana flower bud, sliced
  • 1 bundle of string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 bundle bok choy or pechay
  • 1/2 cup ground toasted rice (or rice flour)
  • 1 cup peanut butter (or ground peanuts)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp annatto powder (for coloring)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong alamang (shrimp paste) for serving
  • Water for boiling

Method

  1. Boil the oxtail in a large pot with water until tender (this may take 2–3 hours or use a pressure cooker for faster results).
  2. In a separate pan, sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the annatto powder and stir well.
  4. Add the tender meat to the sautéed mixture.
  5. Pour in the broth from boiling the meat. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Add peanut butter and mix until well combined. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  7. Add ground toasted rice to thicken the sauce. Stir well.
  8. Add all vegetables and cook until just tender (do not overcook).
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Serve hot with bagoong on the side and steamed rice. Enjoy!
